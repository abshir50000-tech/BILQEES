The Five Pillars of Islam

The Five Pillars are the foundational practices of Islam that every Muslim is expected to perform.

1️⃣ Shahada

Declaration of Faith

"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger."

The foundation of Islam - bearing witness to the oneness of Allah and the prophethood of Muhammad (PBUH).

2️⃣ Salah

Prayer

Five daily prayers performed at specific times throughout the day.

Direct connection with Allah. Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha are the five obligatory prayers.

3️⃣ Zakat

Alms-giving

Annual charity obligation based on wealth and possessions.

Purifies wealth and helps those in need. Typically 2.5% of savings and certain types of wealth.

4️⃣ Sawm

Fasting

Fasting during the holy month of Ramadan from dawn to sunset.

Develops self-discipline, empathy, and spiritual awareness while commemorating the revelation of the Quran.

5️⃣ Hajj

Pilgrimage

Journey to Makkah for the yearly pilgrimage during Dhul-Hijjah.

Performed at least once in a lifetime if physically and financially able. Greatest gathering of Muslims.

Islamic Beliefs (Aqeedah)

Tawhid (Monotheism)

The belief in the absolute oneness of Allah - that there is no god but Allah and He has no partners or associates.

Angels (Malaika)

Belief in the existence of angels created from light who obey Allah's commands and serve specific purposes.

Divine Books

Belief in the scriptures revealed to the prophets, with the Quran being the final and preserved revelation.

Prophets & Messengers

Belief in all prophets sent by Allah, with Muhammad being the final and seal of all prophets.

Day of Judgment

Belief in the coming Day of Judgment when all humans will be held accountable for their deeds.

Divine Decree (Qadr)

Belief in Allah's divine knowledge and wisdom in determining all matters of creation.

Islamic Etiquette (Akhlaq)

Honesty & Truthfulness

Integrity in words and deeds is fundamental to Islamic character. Allah loves the truthful and dislikes liars.

Respect & Kindness

Treating others with respect, compassion, and kindness. "The best among you are those with the best character."

Humility

Being humble and modest while recognizing that all good comes from Allah. Avoid arrogance and pride.

Justice

Upholding justice and fairness in all dealings. Treating everyone equally regardless of status or relationship.

Patience (Sabr)

Enduring hardship with grace and trust in Allah. Patience is greatly rewarded in Islam.

Gratitude (Shukr)

Always being grateful for blessings and acknowledging them as gifts from Allah.
